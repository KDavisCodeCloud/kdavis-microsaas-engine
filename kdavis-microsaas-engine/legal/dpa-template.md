## 1. Purpose

This DPA governs the processing of personal data by the Processor on behalf of the Controller in connection with the Controller's use of the Service, as required by applicable data protection law including GDPR (where applicable) and applicable US state privacy laws.

---

## 2. Definitions

- **Personal Data:** Any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person processed under this Agreement
- **Processing:** Any operation performed on Personal Data
- **Controller:** The entity that determines the purposes and means of Processing (Customer)
- **Processor:** The entity that processes Personal Data on behalf of the Controller (THD Agentic Systems LLC)
- **Sub-processor:** Any third party engaged by the Processor to process Personal Data
- **Data Subject:** The natural person to whom Personal Data relates

---

## 3. Scope and Nature of Processing

**Categories of data subjects:** Controller's end users and employees  
**Categories of personal data:** Names, email addresses, usage data, workflow inputs as submitted by Controller  
**Purpose of processing:** Provision of the Service as described in the Terms of Service  
**Duration:** For the term of the Controller's subscription, plus 30 days post-termination for data export

---

## 4. Processor Obligations

The Processor shall:

a) Process Personal Data only on documented instructions from the Controller, unless required by law  
b) Ensure personnel authorized to process Personal Data are bound by confidentiality obligations  
c) Implement appropriate technical and organizational security measures per Section 7  
d) Assist the Controller in fulfilling data subject rights requests  
e) Delete or return all Personal Data at termination of the Service, at Controller's choice  
f) Make available all information necessary to demonstrate compliance with this DPA  
g) Notify the Controller without undue delay (and within 72 hours where feasible) upon becoming aware of a Personal Data breach

---

## 5. Controller Obligations

The Controller shall:

a) Ensure it has a lawful basis for processing Personal Data under applicable law  
b) Provide all necessary notices to and obtain all necessary consents from data subjects  
c) Ensure Personal Data provided to Processor is accurate and limited to what is necessary  
d) Comply with all applicable data protection laws in its use of the Service

---

## 6. Sub-processors

The Processor is authorized to engage sub-processors as listed in the Privacy Policy. The Processor shall:

- Impose equivalent data protection obligations on all sub-processors
- Remain fully liable for sub-processor performance
- Notify the Controller of any intended changes to sub-processors with reasonable advance notice
- Provide the Controller opportunity to object to new sub-processors

## 7. Security Measures

The Processor implements the following technical and organizational measures:

**Technical:**
- Encryption in transit (TLS 1.2 minimum) and at rest (AES-256)
- Per-tenant data isolation with database row-level security
- Access controls based on least-privilege principle
- Automated threat detection and logging

**Organizational:**
- Access restricted to personnel with business need
- Confidentiality obligations for all personnel with data access
- Incident response procedures with defined escalation paths
- Regular internal security reviews

---

## 8. Data Subject Rights

Where the Processor receives a data subject request directly, it shall:
- Promptly forward the request to the Controller
- Provide reasonable assistance to the Controller in responding
- Not respond directly to data subjects unless authorized by the Controller

---

## 9. Data Breach Notification

In the event of a confirmed Personal Data breach:
- Processor will notify Controller within 72 hours of becoming aware
- Notification will include: nature of breach, categories and approximate number of affected records, likely consequences, measures taken or proposed
- Processor will cooperate fully with Controller's breach response

---

## 10. Data Transfers

Personal Data is processed in the United States. Where Controller is subject to GDPR, transfers are made under Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) as adopted by the European Commission, which are incorporated by reference into this DPA.

---

## 11. Audit Rights

The Controller may, with 30 days written notice and no more than once per year, request:
- Written confirmation of compliance with this DPA
- Results of third-party security audits (where available under NDA)
- On-site audit at Controller's expense (subject to reasonable scheduling)

---

## 12. Term and Termination

This DPA is effective for the duration of the Service subscription. Upon termination:
- Processor will delete all Personal Data within 30 days unless retention is required by law
- Processor will certify deletion in writing upon Controller's request

---

## 13. Governing Law

This DPA is governed by the same law as the underlying Terms of Service.

---

## 14. Order of Precedence

In the event of conflict between this DPA and the Terms of Service, this DPA governs with respect to data protection matters only.
